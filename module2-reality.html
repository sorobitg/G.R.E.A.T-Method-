Module 2: Reality

Coaching Insight

Transformation begins when truth is acknowledged. Your role is to help the client see clearly—without judgment.

Coaching Questions

  • What is happening right now?
  • What patterns do you notice?
  • What have you already tried?

Exercise

Reality Snapshot Journal

This exercise helps you gain an honest, compassionate view of where you are today. Set aside 15–20 quiet minutes, then work through the following prompts in writing:

  1. Describe your current situation.
    In 3–5 sentences, write what is actually happening in the area of your life you want to change. Use facts, not judgments (e.g., "I work 60 hours a week and sleep less than 6 hours a night" rather than "I am a failure at work-life balance").
  2. Identify recurring patterns.
    List two or three patterns you keep seeing in this area of your life. Ask yourself: "When does this show up? What usually triggers it?"
  3. Inventory what you have already tried.
    Write down every action or strategy you have attempted so far. For each one, note briefly why you believe it did or did not work.
  4. Name your feelings without judgment.
    Complete this sentence: "When I look honestly at my current reality, I feel ___________." Acknowledge the emotion, then release any shame or self-criticism attached to it.
  5. Find one truth to build on.
    Write one honest, encouraging statement about a strength, resource, or insight you already possess that can support your forward movement (e.g., "I have overcome challenges before and I have people who care about me").

After completing the prompts, read your responses aloud to yourself or share them with your coach. Notice what shifts when you speak your reality out loud with honesty and grace.

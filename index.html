G — Goal Setting

Clarify what your client truly wants and set powerful, meaningful goals.

▶ Module 1 Lesson Video Paste your Loom embed URL here
💡 Coaching Insight

Goal Setting is the foundation of every powerful coaching conversation. A well-crafted goal is specific, emotionally connected, and aligned with the client's deeper values — not just an outcome on a to-do list.

Key Concepts

  • What does the client really want — beyond the surface goal?
  • Distinguish between outcomes (results) and process goals (actions).
  • Use the GREAT framework to anchor the goal to a bigger purpose.
  • The coach's job is to help the client own the goal fully.

Reflection Questions

🛠 Coaching Exercise

Practice setting a goal for yourself using the GREAT framework. Write a goal that is specific, emotionally meaningful, and connected to your purpose as a coach.

Module 2

R — Reality Check

Explore the current situation with honesty and compassion.

▶ Module 2 Lesson Video Paste your Loom embed URL here
💡 Coaching Insight

The Reality Check step invites the client into honest self-assessment. This is not about problem-solving — it's about witnessing where the client truly stands with curiosity and without judgment.

Key Concepts

  • Create psychological safety before diving into the current reality.
  • Listen for what is not being said — the gap between words and energy.
  • Avoid advice; instead, use open-ended questions to uncover insight.
  • Help the client distinguish between facts, interpretations, and emotions.

Reflection Questions

🛠 Coaching Exercise

Map your own current reality as a coach: Where are you now? What is working, what is challenging, and what do you notice about your mindset?

✝️ Faith Reflection

"The truth will set you free." — John 8:32

Module 3

E — Explore Options

Discover possibilities and strategies to move forward.

▶ Module 3 Lesson Video Paste your Loom embed URL here
💡 Coaching Insight

Exploring options is where possibility thinking comes alive. The coach's role is to expand the client's view — not narrow it. Hold space for creative, unconventional, and courageous ideas.

Key Concepts

  • Brainstorm without filtering — quantity before quality.
  • Use scaling questions: "On a scale of 1–10, how bold are you willing to be?"
  • Challenge limiting beliefs that prevent the client from seeing options.
  • Let the client do the work — resist the urge to suggest solutions.

Reflection Questions

🛠 Coaching Exercise

Generate 10 options for how you could grow as a coach this quarter. Write them without filtering — include wild and realistic ideas alike.

Module 4

A — Action Plan

Create a clear, actionable plan with accountability built in.

▶ Module 4 Lesson Video Paste your Loom embed URL here
💡 Coaching Insight

An action plan bridges insight and impact. Commitment happens when the client chooses their own next steps — not when the coach assigns them. Focus on the smallest meaningful action that moves the needle.

Key Concepts

  • Ask the client to choose — "Which option feels right and bold enough?"
  • Make commitments specific: who, what, when, and how.
  • Build in accountability: "How will you know you've followed through?"
  • Identify obstacles in advance and plan for them.

Reflection Questions

🛠 Coaching Exercise

Write your own action plan for completing this academy. Be specific: what will you do, by when, and how will you stay accountable?

✝️ Faith Reflection

"Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord." — Colossians 3:23

Module 5

T — Transform & Thrive

Support your client in stepping into their fullest potential.

▶ Module 5 Lesson Video Paste your Loom embed URL here
💡 Coaching Insight

Transformation is not a single moment — it's a sustained shift in how the client sees themselves and the world. Your role is to witness, celebrate, and anchor that shift so it becomes permanent.

Key Concepts

  • Acknowledge growth explicitly — clients often don't see how far they've come.
  • Help the client articulate their new identity: "Who are you becoming?"
  • Design rituals or practices that reinforce the transformation.
  • Close the engagement with a powerful celebration of the journey.

Reflection Questions
